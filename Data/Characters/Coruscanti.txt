If for no other reason, the Coruscanti were relieved to see an end of the devastation brought by the Clone Wars. However, the self-anointed Emperor Palpatine made few public appearances, and a number of Coruscanti confessed to having fond memories for Chancellor Valorum.<ref name=\"Tarkin\" /> After the Emperor's [[death]]<ref name=\"Episode VI\">[[Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi|''Star Wars'': Episode VI ''Return of the Jedi'']]</ref> in [[4 ABY]],<ref name=\"Galactic Atlas\" /> Coruscant descended into a state of [[Coruscant civil war|civil war]] as many Coruscanti took to the streets of the [[city]], [[Riot in Monument Plaza|rioting]] in [[Monument Plaza]] to celebrate the end of Palpatine's reign.<ref name=\"Episode VI\" />\n\nThose who identified themselves as Coruscanti often had what some would consider a superior accent, with it being frequently attributed to [[propaganda]] broadcasts, [[Alliance to Restore the Republic|rebel]] satire, and the Imperial elite during the reign of the Empire.<ref name=\"Twilight Company\">''[[Battlefront: Twilight Company]]''</ref> Coruscanti were considered principled, able to verbally parry and weave in debates and politics.<ref name=\"ROUVG\">''[[Star Wars: Rogue One: The Ultimate Visual Guide]]''</ref> According to [[Chief]]tain [[Gruppe]], the human [[Gender|females]] of Coruscant were not known to [[Reproduction|carry or deliver]] their own progeny.
