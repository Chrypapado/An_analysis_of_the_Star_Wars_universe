Unfortunately, Biggs met his death when [[Anakin Skywalker/Legends|Darth Vader]] opened fire on him while he was flanking Luke in the [[DS-1 Orbital Battle Station/Legends|Death Star]] trench.<ref>[[Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope|''Star Wars'': Episode IV ''A New Hope'']]</ref>\n\n==Behind the scenes==\nThe first mention of anyone named Darklighter is in the person of [[Seig Darklighter]], commonly believed to be the leader of the [[Showdown on Coruscant|Jedi Rebellion]], in the [[The Star Wars: Rough Draft|rough draft]] of [[Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope|''Episode IV'']]. The name Darklighter again appears in the [[Adventures of the Starkiller, Episode I: The Star Wars|second draft]]. This draft tells the story of a [[Padawan/Legends|Padawan]] who abandons his [[Jedi Master/Legends|Master]] after coming under the influence of the [[Dark side of the Force/Legends|dark side of the Force]] (referred to as the Bogan).<ref>''[[The Making of Star Wars: The Definitive Story Behind the Original Film]]''</ref>\n\nA man named \"Darklighter\" served at [[Echo Base/Legends|Echo Base]] on the planet [[Hoth/Legends|Hoth]] in [[3 ABY/Legends|3 ABY]]. He [[Battle of Hoth/Legends|evacuated]] Hoth aboard the [[GR-75 medium transport/Legends|GR-75 medium transport]] [[Bright Hope/Legends|''Bright Hope'']], the final transport to escape the planet. After the transport was disabled trying to run the [[Galactic Empire/Legends|Imperial]] blockade, he reached the planet [[Darlyn Boda/Legends|Darlyn Boda]] with the other surviving members of the ship, before being one of just six of those survivors to finally reach the secret [[Haven (rendezvous point)|Rebel rendezvous point]] due to his importance to the Rebellion. He served aboard the [[EF76 Nebulon-B escort frigate/Legends|EF76 Nebulon-B escort frigate]] [[Redemption/Legends|''Redemption'']] immediately thereafter.
