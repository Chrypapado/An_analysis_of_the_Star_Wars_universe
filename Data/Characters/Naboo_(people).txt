You must understand this.|[[Obi-Wan Kenobi]], to [[Rugor Nass]]|Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace}}\nThe '''Naboo''' (singular \"Naboo\"), also known as '''Naboolians''', were a cultural group of [[human]]s from the [[planet]] [[Naboo]].<ref name=\"TPM\" /> Contrary to the [[Homeworld|native]] [[Gungan]]s, with whom they shared their world, the Naboo originated offworld as human [[colonist]]s from [[Grizmallt]].<ref name=\"Crisis\">{{SW|url=series/clone-wars/crisis-on-naboo-trivia-gallery|text=Crisis on Naboo Trivia Gallery}} {{C|slides 2-3 captions}}</ref> The Naboo [[Noble|aristocrats]] were known for their [[Clothing|sartorial]] extravagance.<ref name=\"Tark\">[[Tarkin (novel)|''Tarkin'']]</ref> Unlike the Gungans, who were a proud warrior [[species]], the human colonists of Naboo were peaceful. This contrast of ideology caused tension between the two civilizations for [[Standard century|centuries]].<ref name=\"SWE Gungan Grand Army\">{{SWE|groups|gungangrandarmy|Gungan Grand Army}}</ref>\n\n==Society and culture==\nThe Naboo valued culture and beauty, which was reflected in their everyday [[Clothing|attire]]. [[Youngling|Children]] wore graceful clothes made of natural materials.<ref name=\"VE\">''[[Star Wars: The Visual Encyclopedia]]''</ref>\n\n==History==\nThe Naboo originated as a group of [[human]] colonists who fled the war-torn [[Core Worlds|Core World]] of [[Grizmallt]] thousands of [[Standard year|years]] before the [[Clone Wars]].<ref name=\"Rise\">''[[Rise of the Separatists]]''</ref> Led by [[Kwilaan]], the colonists crashed on the [[homeworld]] of the [[Gungan]] species and established their presence there. They ventured into the [[Gallo Mountains]] and created a [[farm]]ing community at [[Dee'ja Peak]].<ref name=\"Crisis\" />\n\nAt first, the Gungans tried to avoid contact with the colonists, retreating in the planet's waterways. When the humans and Gungans finally met, however, their relations soon turned to hostility. After fighting over territory and resources for a long time, the two species eventually resolved to simply live apart and ignore each other.<ref name=\"Rise\" />\n\nFollowing the end of their conflicts with the Gungans, the Naboo had to deal with internal wars, as their various city-states broke into factions and engaged in open warfare.
